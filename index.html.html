About Purulia

Discover Purulia's rich history, geography and culture

Geography & Climate

Purulia is located in the western part of West Bengal, featuring undulating terrain with lateritic soil. The district enjoys a moderate climate with hot summers (25-45°C), cool winters (7-20°C) and moderate rainfall (1100-1500mm annually).

The district is known for its scenic hills like Ayodhya, Panchet and Baghmundi ranges, along with numerous waterfalls including Joychandi and Hundru falls.

History & Culture

Purulia has a rich historical legacy dating back to ancient times, evident in its archaeological sites and medieval temples. The region is famous for its indigenous tribal cultures and the UNESCO-recognized Chhau dance.

The district has a predominantly tribal population including Santhals, Bhumij and Munda communities who maintain their traditional lifestyles.

Economy & Tourism

Purulia's economy is primarily agrarian with rice as the main crop. The district is also known for sericulture, handicrafts and mining. Tourism is growing rapidly due to Purulia's natural beauty, cultural heritage and proximity to Jharkhand.
